## Briefing

On 01 April 2026, our Security Operations Center detected and stopped a targeted cyberattack against our internal systems. An external attacker from IP address `10.0.2.20` sent a fraudulent email that tricked one employee into revealing their login credentials. Using those credentials, the attacker gained access to a vulnerable internal server and attempted to take full control of it.

Our automated security system detected the attack and blocked the attacker's IP address within 47 seconds of confirmation. The compromised server was isolated from the rest of the network, and the affected employee accounts were locked and reset. The attacker was fully evicted by 11:30 AM — approximately 3.5 hours after our first alert.

No customer data was accessed or exfiltrated. The incident is fully contained.

**Three immediate actions have been taken:** the attacker has been blocked, compromised accounts have been secured, and the vulnerable server has been patched.

**Two priority improvements are underway:** enabling two-factor login for all staff accounts (within 7 days), and updating our email filtering to catch future phishing attempts automatically (within 30 days).

We will provide a follow-up update confirming completion of these improvements.
